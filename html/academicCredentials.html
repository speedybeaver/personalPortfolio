My Favorite Courses

GAT

Geometry and Algebra with Transformations, or GAT, is the 9th grade math course at MMSTC. This rigorous course challenges my mathmatical abilities and puts me in an environment where I get to make new connections and learn new things about math every day. Mr. Acre, the teacher of this course, talks to me about topics that we may not have discussed in class, and always makes me feel welcome to ask him questions about things I don't understand.

American History

The contents of the American History class do not particularly align with my more math-oriented mind, but Mr. Cutlip, the teacher of the class, makes it a very interesting class where we get to discuss global issues every day and learn about finances, laws, and other important things.

IDS

Interdiciplinary Studies, or IDS, is an abstract course in MMSTC. Being an technology-based arts class, we learn a wide variety of technological skills such as web design, CAD, Microsoft Excel/Google Sheets, digital presentation skills, and are given the oppurtnity and time to explore our options for the future, such as careers that we may want to pursue and colleges we may want to attend. The teacher, Ms. Gravel, makes this class all the more enjoyable as she always has a smile on her face when we walk into the classroom.
